Writing Up the Documentation to Your Code

Apart from the comments and general structure of your code, the quality of the documentation you write is of crucial importance, because to begin with your code will have to be integrated into the MAGNUS system, and later it may become necessary or desirable to modify or extend it or localise later bugs.

Roughly, your documentation for each part of your implementation should be divided into two parts: a general part and a reference part.

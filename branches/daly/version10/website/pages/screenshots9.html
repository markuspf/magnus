An Example Session

The Tools Menu

Magnus structures the session under the assumption that you are given a group on the workbench and you want to ask questions about the group. It structures the questions into a natural progression and separates these questions by "kind" into different levels of menu structure.

At the first level of menu structure you may want to ask about properties of the group (is questions). For example, you might want to know if the group is trivial or free abelian.

You may want to perform a computation with the group (compute questions). For example, you might want to compute the order of the group or the torsion-free rank of the group.

You may want to find a different representation of the group (find question). For example, you might want to know if the group can be rewritten as a permutation.

You may want to look at the words of the group (enumerate questions). For example, you might want to look at all of the subgroups.

You might just want to remove the group and all results that are left around on the workspace that have been derived from this group. The delete menu item will do that.

Once we have selected the group A1 the first level of the tools menu looks like:

      Is A1 ...
      Compute ...
      Find ...
      Enumerate ...
      Delete selected objects and dependencies
      Synopsis

The Subgroup Problem (aka Generalized Word Problem)

Given a finite presentation P of a group G there are words U, V, W, ... defining elements u, v, w of G. Define H = Grp(u,v,w,...). The Subgroup Problem, also known as the generalized word problem, is to decide if a word v is in H. An extension to this problem is to express v explicitly as a product of the elements of H.

There is no general algorithm to solve this problem for all finite presentations. Magnus has several algorithms which can be applied to a finite presentation to attempt to decide this question for a particular finite presentation.

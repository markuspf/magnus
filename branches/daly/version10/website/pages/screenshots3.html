How to work with Magnus

Magnus works with groups as primitive objects. Since the groups are usually given by a finite presentation there is little that is known about the group structure. Magnus is a place to explore that structure using a set of "Tools".

Each group you want to examine gets put on the workbench using the "Check-in" menu. When the group is checked-in it is given a name and an icon. The named icon is placed on the workbench.

Since there are a variety of different ways to write the presentation for a group and a variety of groups the "Check-in" menu presents a list of groups Magnus understands:

      Abelian Group
      Amalgamated Product
      Finitely Presented Group
      Free Group
      Free Nilpotent Group
      HNN-extension
      Nilpotent Group
      One Relator Group
      Small Cancellation Group
      Group Definition Templates
      Permutation
      Synopsis
